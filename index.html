How is this calculated? (v1 — honest about its limits)

Income mode: household income is converted from your currency into international dollars (PPP) — what the money actually buys — then divided by the number of people supported by that income. That per-person amount is placed on a global per-person income model.

The current model is still provisional: a log-normal body with rough anchors ($3,000/yr median and about $55,000/yr at the 99th percentile, PPP per person), then a Pareto (Fermi) estimate above the top 1%. These anchors are placeholders until we replace them with World Bank PIP percentile data.

Wealth mode: net worth is compared in nominal USD against UBS Global Wealth Report 2025 wealth bands for adults at the end of 2024. Inside each broad band, the app interpolates; above billionaire territory, it uses a small Fermi tail. Wealth is a stock, not income, so this is a separate comparison.

"Combined income/net worth of the N poorest" line: in income mode this divides your per-person PPP income by a Fermi floor of int$ 1/day (≈ $365/yr) for the very poorest — surveys cannot resolve that tail, and the log-normal body would otherwise assign them unrealistically tiny incomes and inflate the count. In wealth mode it integrates the model's bottom band (net worth spread evenly from $0 to $10k) and ignores negative net worth, so the real count is likely higher. Both are order-of-magnitude estimates, not facts.

"The top person = N of you" line: this uses two reference anchors that are separate from the distribution model. Income mode compares you against int$ 3 billion/yr — a Fermi anchor for the largest documented single-year personal paydays (top hedge-fund managers in exceptional years, per the Institutional Investor "Rich List" / Forbes; nobody has a salary like this). Wealth mode compares you against roughly US$400 billion, the scale of the richest individual's net worth on the Forbes / Bloomberg lists as of 2025 — a number that moves daily with markets. Order of magnitude only.

Text vs Visual view: both are rendered from the same computation — the Visual view only changes presentation. Its big numbers are rounded to at most 2 significant digits, and in the figure charts one figure stands for 1, 10, 100, … people (the key under each chart says which), so the pictograms never imply more precision than the model has.

This is a v1 approximation: gross pre-tax income, rough PPP factors, broad wealth bands, and adjustable tails. Numbers will move as we refine the data, currency handling, and tax treatment.
